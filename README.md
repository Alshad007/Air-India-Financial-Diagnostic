# Air India Financial Diagnostic | Unit Economics Benchmarking vs IndiGo (FY2023-24)

## Project Overview

A post-privatisation profitability study of Air India, benchmarking its unit economics against IndiGo using publicly available annual report data. The analysis identifies the primary drivers of Air India's cost disadvantage and formulates strategic recommendations to close the gap by FY2027.

---

## Key Finding

> **Air India's CASK of ₹6.10 is ~39% higher than IndiGo's ₹4.38** — the single largest driver of Air India's profitability gap post-privatisation.

---

## Metrics Analysed

| Metric | Air India | IndiGo |
|---|---|---|
| CASK (Cost per Available Seat Kilometre) | ₹6.10 | ₹4.38 |
| RASK (Revenue per Available Seat Kilometre) | ₹5.24 | ₹4.96 |
| Load Factor | 86.1% | 85.9% |
| Yield per RPK | ₹5.71 | ₹5.08 |
| Operating Margin | -11.1% | +8.3% |

---

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Fleet Modernisation** — Replace aging aircraft to reduce fuel and maintenance costs
2. **International Route Expansion** — Leverage Air India's 12% yield advantage on international routes
3. **Hub Consolidation** — Optimise operations around key hubs to reduce per-unit costs

**Projected outcome:** CASK reduction to below ₹5.00 by FY2027, generating annual savings of ₹12,000–15,000 Mn
